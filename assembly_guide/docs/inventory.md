## Tools and other things you'll need

- Soldering iron and solder specifically for electronics
- Wire stripper or cutters
- 9v battery
- Tape

## Electronics

[![Space Dice electronic components](/img/electronic_components.jpg)](/img/electronic_components.jpg)

Of course, you'll need the [Space Dice DIY Electronics Kit](https://www.oskitone.com/product/space-dice-diy-electronics-kit). 32 pieces:

- 1 PCB
- 3 IC chips
- 3 IC chip sockets
- 4 switches: 1 tactile, 2 vertical, 1 right-angle
- 2 electrolytic capacitors
- 3 ceramic capacitors
- 1 speaker
- 6 LEDs
- 3 resistors
- 3 potentiometers
- 1 9v battery snap
- 1 nut
- 1 bolt

## 3D Prints

[![Space Dice 3D-printed parts](/img/3d_printed_parts.jpg)](/img/3d_printed_parts.jpg)

The [3D-printed parts](3d-printing-parts-and-slicing) aren't technically required to make your Space Dice, but, of course, it's not nearly as fun without them. 12 pieces:

- 1 enclosure bottom
- 1 enclosure top
- 3 knobs
- 1 button lever + 1 actuator mount
- 1 LED display
- 1 side switch clutch
- 2 top switch clutches
- 1 speaker grill battery cover

### Recommended

- Needle nose pliers
- PCB vice or “helping hands” holder
- “Solder sucker” or desoldering braid
- Multimeter for debugging
- Oscilloscope for learning
- Patience, patience, patience
