## Steps

1. Take a moment to examine your 3D prints and cut/brush/pull off any plastic stringing from the printing process.
2. Prep enclosure bottom
   1. Use a thin screw driver, pen tip, or similar tool to break away the support material at the top of the nut lock on the enclosure bottom.  
      [![breaking away nut lock support material](/img/breaking_away_nut_lock_support_material.jpg)](/img/breaking_away_nut_lock_support_material.jpg)
   2. Slide the square nut into its cavity. It'll be snug, but it _will_ fit! Needle-nose pliers can help.  
      [![square nut in cavity](/img/square_nut_in_cavity.jpg)](/img/square_nut_in_cavity.jpg)
3. Prep enclosure top
   1. Slide the button cap into its fixture in the enclosure top, then gently pop it up into place.  
      [![button cap in enclosure top fixture](/img/button_cap_in_enclosure_top_fixture.jpg)](/img/button_cap_in_enclosure_top_fixture.jpg)
   2. It's okay if it's a little loose. It will be held in place when fully assembled.  
      [![button cap positioned](/img/button_cap_positioned.jpg)](/img/button_cap_positioned.jpg)

Not working as expected? Check the [Assembly troubleshooting](assembly-troubleshooting.md) section. Otherwise, continue.
